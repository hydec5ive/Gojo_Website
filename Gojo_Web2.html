Exploring Blockchain & Web3

Cryptocurrency is digital money that doesn’t rely on banks or governments. Instead, it uses blockchain, a special system that acts like a public record book or ledger. Imagine a notebook everyone can see but no one can erase. Every time someone sends or receives crypto, it’s written down on the blockchain, and becomes immutable. This makes it decentralized, meaning no single person controls it, and it’s super hard to hack. I got hooked on this idea because it’s futuristic and ties into my love for tech, especially living in Shenzhen, where innovation is everywhere. During my DS2500 course, I worked on a group project where we built a model to predict Bitcoin’s price. We used past price data, trends, and machine learning models to guess what might happen next. It was exciting—our model looked promising at first, crunching numbers like a pro. But it failed in the end. Why? Predicting the future, especially with stocks or crypto, is nearly impossible—prices swing wildly due to news, hype, or even tweets! It taught me how unpredictable this world is, but it only fueled my curiosity more.

Key Web3 Concepts

  • Decentralization: No single authority controls the system.
  • Smart Contracts: Automated, trustworthy agreements.
  • DeFi: Finance without traditional banks.
  • NFTs: Unique digital assets on the blockchain.
  • DAOs: Community-run organizations without bosses.
